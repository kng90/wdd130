Overview

Purpose

Dogs Doing Jobs is a playful, informative website that explores the hardworking world of real and imaginary canine careers. From bomb-sniffing heroes to couch supervisors, it celebrates the charm and usefulness of dogs in every role. The site combines humor, fun facts, and adorable visuals to entertain and educate.

Audience

This website is designed for middle and high school students who love dogs and enjoy a creative, lighthearted learning experience. It’s perfect for curious minds with a sense of humor.

Content

Home page

Site Purpose
Welcome to Fetch & Frolic! This isn’t just another dog website. This is where dogs work, play, and occasionally nap on the job. Fetch & Frolic is your go-to destination for a good laugh, a soft "awww," and the kind of canine chaos that comes from imagining dogs with full-time jobs and huge personalities.

Why I Built This
I were inspired by the idea that dogs might just be better at our jobs than humans are—and a whole lot cuter doing them. From Barketing Managers to Chief Nap Officers, I started imagining what the workplace would look like if dogs ran the show. It got a little silly.

Audience and Tone
This site is made for dog lovers, overworked humans, and anyone who needs a break from grown-up responsibilities. If you’ve ever wished your coworkers had more fur and fewer opinions, you’re in the right place.

Site Navigation Overview
On this site, you’ll find:
1. Funny Dog Jobs – Meet the working dogs living their best lives.
2. Real Dogs Doing Jobs – A spotlight on incredible real-life working dogs.

Funny Dog Jobs

Workplace Setup
Dogs with jobs? You bet. Not just service dogs or police pups—we’re talking career dogs with business cards, headshots, and an inflated sense of self-worth. This page is where you'll meet the Barkforce.

Pawffice Manager – Barley
Barley keeps the team on track mostly by wagging his tail encouragingly and nudging coworkers for belly rubs. He’s never sent a Slack message, but everyone still listens to him.

Chief Snack Officer – Mochi
Mochi is responsible for taste-testing every snack on-site. She takes her job very seriously and often “accidentally” eats other people's lunches. It’s fine—she’s in charge of snacks.

Lead Zoomie Strategist – Luna
Luna has engineered the perfect office route for maximum zoomie velocity. Her hallway sprints are inspirational and mildly terrifying.

Head of Barketing – Rusty
Rusty runs our social media presence with plenty of flair and howling commentary. His latest campaign, “Bark Like You Mean It,” got over 3 likes. Big success.

Team Benefits
All team members enjoy unlimited nap breaks, stock options in squeaky toys, paid paw-ternity leave, and at least one treat per hour.

Real Dogs Doing Jobs

Why We Love Working Dogs
While our imaginary workplace pups are fun, real dogs are out there doing incredible jobs every day. From guiding the visually impaired to detecting illness, these dogs aren’t just good—they’re amazing.

Guide Dogs
Guide dogs help people who are blind or visually impaired navigate the world safely. They stay calm in crowds, ignore distractions, and even disobey dangerous commands if needed.

Therapy Dogs
Therapy dogs provide comfort in hospitals, schools, and disaster areas. They help lower stress, ease anxiety, and bring smiles to people who need it most.

Search and Rescue Dogs
From natural disasters to missing person cases, SAR dogs use their sense of smell and stamina to find people.

Detection Dogs
Detection dogs sniff out everything from explosives to diseases. They’re the scientists of the dog world—except much cuter.

Police and Military Dogs
K9 units assist police and military teams with tracking, searching, and suspect apprehension.

Retirement and Recognition
Working dogs retire too—often to loving homes where they get to nap guilt-free. Some are honored for their service.

Support Working Dogs
Want to help? Donate to service dog orgs or raise a future guide pup!
